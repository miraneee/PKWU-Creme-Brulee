About Crème Brûlée

Royal Origins

Crème brûlée, meaning "burnt cream" in French, is a rich custard dessert topped with a layer of hardened caramelized sugar. This elegant dessert has graced royal tables since the 17th century.

The Perfect Torch

The signature caramelized top is created by sprinkling sugar over the custard and using a kitchen torch to create that satisfying crack when broken with a spoon.

Timeless Appeal

From Parisian bistros to modern kitchens worldwide, crème brûlée remains one of the most beloved and sophisticated desserts, perfect for any special occasion.

A Rich History

The origins of crème brûlée are debated among culinary historians, with France, England, and Spain all claiming to be its birthplace.
